About Philippine Music

Philippine music is a blend of indigenous traditions, Spanish and American influences, and modern global styles. From folk rituals to P-pop and hip-hop, Filipino artists continue to create a unique, evolving sound.

Major Types of Philippine Music

Philippine music can be grouped into several main categories:

  • Traditional & Folk Music – rooted in community rituals and daily life.
  • Religious & Liturgical Music – used in church services and fiestas.
  • Classical & Art Music – concert works by Filipino composers.
  • Original Pilipino Music (OPM) – mainstream pop, rock, and ballads.
  • Contemporary & Indie – experimental, electronic, and alternative sounds.

Everyday Music Experiences

Philippine Music Through the Years

The table below shows how styles changed over time:

Era / Period Main Genres Key Features
Pre-colonial & Indigenous Folk, Ritual Music Use of instruments like kulintang, kubing, and nose flute; music tied to community life.
Spanish Colonial Period Kundiman, Harana, Church Music Romantic serenades, devotional songs, and European-style harmony.
American & Post-war Era Big Band, Jazz, Manila Sound English lyrics, radio hits, and early pop influences.
Modern OPM (1970s–2000s) Pop, Rock, Ballad Band culture, powerful ballads, and iconic OPM classics.
Contemporary OPM (2010s–Present) Indie, Hip-hop, R&B, P-pop Digital production, social media fandoms, and global collaboration.

Core Elements of Philippine Music

Melody
Often lyrical and expressive, making songs easy to sing along with.
Lyrics
Written in Filipino, English, or regional languages; themes of love, family, faith, and real-life struggles are common.
Rhythm
Ranges from gentle, flowing ballads to upbeat dance and festival grooves.
Instruments
Combines traditional instruments (kulintang, bandurria) with modern ones (guitar, drums, keyboards, synthesizers).
